Step-by-Step: How to Apply for Transitional Housing (Foster Youth)

  1. Contact your ILP (Independent Living Program) Coordinator or social worker.
  2. Ask if you qualify for THP-NMD (if extended foster care) or THP-Plus (after exiting care).
  3. Request a list of approved housing providers in San Diego County.
  4. Gather documents:
    • State ID
    • Social Security card
    • Foster verification letter
    • Education or employment plan
  5. Call housing providers directly and ask:
    • Am I eligible?
    • Is there a waitlist?
    • What paperwork is required?
    • What are next steps?

What If I Get Denied?

Getting denied does NOT mean you're out of options. Housing programs have limited space and strict funding rules. It is not a judgment about you.

Step 1: Ask Why

  • Was it eligibility?
  • Missing paperwork?
  • No openings right now?
  • Age or funding limits?

Ask if you can reapply later or be added to a waitlist.

Step 2: Ask for Referrals

Even if they can’t take you, ask:

  • “Who else should I call?”
  • “Is there a similar program nearby?”

Step 3: Contact 2-1-1

Call 2-1-1 in San Diego County for coordinated entry and emergency housing referrals.

211 San Diego Housing Help

Step 4: Talk to Your ILP / Social Worker / CASA

They can advocate for you, help appeal decisions, or connect you to emergency funding.

You are allowed to ask for help more than once.
